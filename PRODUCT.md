# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Owner-operators and sales/ops leads at small trades and shop businesses, evaluating whether to hand phone/lead-response duties to an AI vendor. Five named ICPs get dedicated spoke pages: solar installers (post-25D contraction, racing shared leads), roofing companies (storm-surge/insurance-claim intake), tattoo studios (desk-not-design, deposit/no-show), cannabis dispensaries + accessories retailers (deplatformed-channel, compliance-heavy), and sales organizations (voice agents for the floor). Secondary/legacy trades still served: HVAC, plumbing, landscaping, barbershops, classic-car shops. Context: mobile-heavy, time-pressured, skeptical of "AI slop" and cheap-bot vendors, burned before by vague pricing or over-promised automation. A missed call is lost revenue for this audience, not an abstraction.

## Product Purpose

Gaelworx is an AI automation agency selling five fixed-scope services: managed AI voice agents (answer/qualify/book every call), workflow automations, custom-built software (owned outright, not leased), cinematic websites, and AI installation (standing up managed agent stacks — OpenClaw, CrewAI, LangChain, n8n — for a team). The site's job is to convert trade-business visitors into booked intro calls (or DEPLOY for buy-now services) by naming the fixed price before work begins and proving the fit for their specific vertical. Success = message-matched spoke pages that win narrow, high-intent searches ("AI receptionist for roofers") and a homepage journey that converts broad traffic.

## Brand Personality

Fire & iron. A forge/metalworking metaphor carried literally: Grenze Gotisch display type, dark theme (never brightened "for legibility"), molten/knotwork motifs, Latin motto "Non sine periculo" (not without danger). Voice: forward-deployed engineer, not SaaS vendor — "a decade on the sales floor," "bedrock, not frontier finger-crossing." Confident, plainspoken, unafraid of danger/edge framing; earns trust through fixed pricing and named tradeoffs, never through hype or fabricated authority (no invented certifications, no vanity stats — "stat discipline": only sourced, sanctioned numbers reach copy).

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS-cream AI-agency sites (cream/beige body bg, gradient-text headlines, glassmorphism cards, hero-metric templates, tiny uppercase eyebrows). Cheap robocall/spam-adjacent voice-vendor positioning (TCPA-blind, purchased lists, no consent posture). Fabricated or unconfirmed certification claims. Vanity/hardcoded stats presented as live data. Over-claiming features the underlying product doesn't have (e.g. do not imply live GPS tracking, geofenced clock-in, or production scheduling that isn't real).

## Design Principles

- Fixed price named before work begins, balance billed on execution — pricing transparency is the core trust mechanism, not a footnote.
- Message-match over mega-page: each ICP gets its own spoke with its own enemy, proof, and CTA; never collapse verticals into one generic page.
- Fire & iron is locked identity, not a mood — never soften, brighten, or genericize it "so it reads better."
- Stat discipline: every number in copy must be sourced and sanctioned; no invented credentials, no hardcoded vanity metrics.
- One decisive, well-scoped fix beats a stack of small unilateral tweaks — respect prior locked decisions rather than re-litigating them piecemeal.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA site-wide, stated and audited (see accessibility.html): real HTML text with logical heading order, landmarks on every page, measured body-text contrast (5.2:1–15.9:1 in practice), keyboard/screen-reader support, and `prefers-reduced-motion` honored (animation fully disabled, pinned/scrolljacked sections flatten to static readable layout).
