This site is heavy on fire and motion by design. It is also built so that none of that gets between you and what you came for. This page says what we hold ourselves to, what is already in place, where the edges are, and exactly how to reach a human if something is in your way.

Our standard

We aim for conformance with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2, Level AA, across every page of this site. Accessibility is treated as part of the build, not a patch afterward: new pages and features are checked against the same standard before they ship.

What is in place

Every page can be operated by keyboard alone, with a visible amber focus indicator on links, buttons, form fields, and accordions, and a skip-to-content link at the top of every page. The menu is a proper dialog: focus is trapped while it is open, Escape closes it, and focus returns to the button that opened it.

Text contrast is measured, not guessed: body text runs from 5.2:1 to 15.9:1 against its backgrounds, and interface inks meet WCAG AA at their sizes. Content is real HTML text, headings follow a logical order, and every page carries proper landmarks (navigation, main content, footer).

If your system asks for reduced motion, this site listens: scroll-driven scenes flatten into a plain readable page, ambient animation stops, and nothing is hidden behind an effect. The 3D coin is decorative; it has a static image fallback and is skipped by screen readers. Interactive tools (calculators, the audit, the pricing chooser) expose their state to assistive technology, and sliders announce their values in dollars, not raw numbers.

Where the edges are

The homepage is a scroll-driven, animated journey; with reduced motion off, it is the most visually intense page here. Every fact and every way to contact us on it also exists as plain text on quieter pages, and the journey itself honors reduced-motion preferences completely. We review the site against WCAG ourselves; it has not yet had a third-party audit, and we will not claim one until it has.

If something is in your way

Tell us and we will fix it, quickly. Email hello@gaelworx.com with the page and what happened, or call +1 (369) 212-1203 and say it out loud. Accessibility reports go to the people who write the code, not a ticket queue.
