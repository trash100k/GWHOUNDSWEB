HOW MUCH DOES AN
AI RECEPTIONIST COST?

Short answer: self-serve AI receptionist tools run about $25–$199 a month, and you build the call flows yourself. A managed AI voice agent — where someone builds, trains, and runs it for you — starts at $899/month plus a one-time setup, priced to replace a job you'd otherwise lose to a missed call, not just a software subscription.

The two tiers, side by side

Every AI receptionist on the market falls into one of two tiers: a self-serve tool you configure yourself, or a managed build where someone else does the setup, tuning, and ongoing management. The table below compares them honestly — neither is a scam, they solve different problems.

Why the number is higher than a $29 chatbot

A cheap self-serve tool gives you the software and leaves the rest to you: writing the script, handling the edge cases, noticing when it's giving a bad answer, fixing it. A managed agent is priced to include the labor, not just the license — someone who has spent a decade on a real sales floor tunes the flow, the objection handling, and the tone so it actually converts instead of sounding like a robot reading a card, and keeps tuning it as your business changes.

The honest way to think about the gap: the $899/mo isn't competing with a $29/mo chatbot subscription. It's competing with the cost of a missed call — a job that goes to whichever business answered the phone first.

What drives the price up or down

Within the managed tier, three things move the number: call volume (minutes beyond the included 3,000/mo bill at $0.30/min), how many separate call flows you need (a business answering for multiple lines or departments costs more to set up than a single front desk), and whether you want inbound only or outbound dialing added (outbound campaigns run on the same per-minute and per-appointment structure). None of it is hidden — the first call scopes the exact number before anything is built.

What does a managed AI voice agent cost per month?

A Gaelworx managed AI voice agent starts at $899/mo per agent, which includes 3,000 minutes a month (then $0.30/min after that), plus a one-time $2,500 setup and a $50-per-booked-appointment performance line. Every number is public on the pricing page.

Is there a setup fee?

Yes. A managed voice agent has a one-time $2,500 setup — scoping the call flows, wiring the CRM and calendar, and tuning the voice — before the $899/mo running cost starts. Self-serve tools usually skip a setup fee because you configure it yourself.

What's the cheapest way to get an AI receptionist?

Self-serve AI receptionist and voice-agent tools generally run $25 to $199 a month, with no setup fee, because you build the call flows and prompts yourself. That's the right call if you have the time to configure and maintain it. A managed agent costs more because someone else builds, tunes, and runs it for you.

Does the price change if I get more calls?

With Gaelworx, the $899/mo includes 3,000 minutes; minutes beyond that bill at $0.30/min, and there's a $50 fee per appointment the agent actually books. Most self-serve tools bill similarly by usage tier or per-minute overage once you exceed the plan's included minutes.
