ASKED BEFORE THE JOB.

ALL ANSWERS →
Can it survive the call surge after a hailstorm?

Yes. The surge is what it was forged for. The agent takes every call at once: no hold queue, no busy signal, no temp hired the week the wave breaks. Each caller gets triaged, their claim facts captured, and an inspection booked in the order the storm sent them. Answer a lead inside five minutes and you book the job at roughly nine times the rate. The agent answers on the first ring.

Does it write into AccuLynx, JobNimbus, or Roofr?

We wire the agent into the CRM you already run: AccuLynx, JobNimbus, Roofr, Leap, or CompanyCam workflows. The inspection lands on your calendar, the claim facts land on the job record, and the transcript is yours to read. No separate inbox. No swivel-chair data entry.

Will it give homeowners insurance advice?

No, and that's deliberate. The agent takes claim facts: address, carrier, whether a claim is filed, adjuster status, damage type, roof age. It never negotiates with a carrier, never touches a deductible, and never promises what a policy will pay. You set the boundaries when we scope the build, and every word it says sits in a transcript you can read. Facts in. Advice never out.

Won't a homeowner with a hole in her roof demand a human?

She demands an answer. The agent says it's an AI up front, takes the damage, the address, and the fear seriously, and books the inspection on the spot. A true emergency hands off to you live, mid-call. What she will not do is leave a voicemail and wait: she dials the next roofer on the list. Voicemail loses her. An answer keeps her.
