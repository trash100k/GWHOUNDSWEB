Will buyers know they're talking to an AI?

Yes. The agent says what it is on every call. It never pretends to be one of your reps, never dials a bought list, and never plays the fake-rep game buyers learned to smell in one sentence. Its job is honest and narrow: answer the inbound lead fast, ask your qualifying questions, book the meeting. Your humans stay the closers. Buyers punish imitation. They don't punish a fast, straight answer.

Does the sales voice agent work with our CRM and calendar?

It wires into HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, and Close, and books through the calendar your team already runs, Calendly included. Every call is logged and transcribed into the record before the rep's next dial: no sticky notes, no 'I'll log it Friday', no forecast built on calls that officially never happened. If your stack is stranger than that, we build the connector. That's the workshop's job.

Is outbound AI calling going to get us sued?

Outbound is consent-based or it doesn't ship: your list, your opt-ins, an agent that identifies itself, opt-outs honored on the spot and logged. Inbound carries none of that baggage, because the lead called you. Anyone promising blast-dialing from rotating numbers is selling you the lawsuit along with the software.
